1. Homepage: The "Power Suit" Entrance
The goal here is branding and quick navigation into categories.

Header: Sticky Nav with [Logo] | [Suits] [Shirts] [Accessories] | [Search] [Account/Profile] [Cart (0)].

Hero Section: High-resolution image of a lawyer in a courtroom or office.

Headline: "Dress for the Verdict."

CTA: [Shop the Collection] [Book a Virtual Tailor].

Curated Categories: Three large cards: "The Courtroom Classic," "The Boardroom Power Move," and "Essential Accessories."

Social Proof: A "Trusted by Partners at [Law Firm Logos]" bar to build immediate prestige.

The Form Layout (Above the Fold or Mid-Page)
Section Header: "The At-Home Atelier: Professional Tailoring at Your Doorstep."

Step 1: Select Your Interest: (Dropdown) e.g., Three-Piece Suit, Tuxedo, Corporate Daily.

Step 2: Fitting Address: (Auto-complete via Google Maps API).

Step 3: Schedule: A calendar widget showing available dates and time slots.

Step 4: Contact Info: Name, Email, Phone.

The "Pay & Book" Button: This triggers the Razorpay flow immediately.

2. Product Listing Page (PLP)
This is where your Postgres filters (Fabric, Fit, Color) come to life.

Left Sidebar (Filters): * Fit (Slim, Modern, Classic).

Fabric (Wool, Linen, Silk Blend).

Price Range (using your base_price field).

Product Grid: 2 or 3 columns.

Card Content: Primary Image, Hover for second image, Name, Price, and a small Rating Star (from your avg_rating field).

Quick Add: A "Quick Buy" button that opens a small overlay for size selection.

3. Product Detail Page (PDP): The Conversion Point
This page needs to handle the complex variants we discussed in the schema.

Left Side: Vertical thumbnail list + Large main image viewer.

Right Side:

Product Title & Price.

Size Selection: A dropdown or tile list.

"Find My Fit" Tool: A link that opens a modal to the Measurement Profile.

Add to Cart / Wishlist.

Fabric Details: Pulling from your attributes table (e.g., "Super 120s Italian Wool").

Below the Fold:

"Complete the Look": Cross-sell ties, cufflinks, or pocket squares that go with this specific suit.

Reviews & Ratings: Displaying the comments, verified purchase badges, and admin replies.

4. The "Measurement Vault" (User Profile)
This is your "sticky" feature. Once a lawyer saves their measurements here, they are locked into your ecosystem.

Layout: A clean, form-based dashboard.

Input Fields: [Neck] [Chest] [Waist] [Inseam] [Sleeve].

Visual Aid: Small diagrams showing exactly where to measure.

Status: "Verified by Admin" badge (linked to your is_verified_by_tailor boolean).

5. Checkout: The Razorpay Handshake
Keep this single-page to reduce drop-offs.

Column 1: Shipping & Billing: Pulling from user_addresses.

Column 2: Order Summary: * List of items with unit_price.

Discount Code Input: (Applies the logic to your coupons table).

Subtotal, Shipping, Tax, and Grand Total.

Column 3: Payment: * "Proceed to Secure Payment" button (Triggers the Razorpay Modal).

Security badges (SSL, Razorpay Secured).
