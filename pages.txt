1. Homepage: The "Digital Atelier"
Goal: Establish brand authority and drive the "Home Measurement" VIP service.

Hero Section: * Full-width cinematic image of a sharp suit in a law library setting.

Overlay Text: "The Verdict is In: Precision Tailoring for the Modern Counsel."

Primary CTA: [Book Home Measurement] (High-contrast button).

Secondary CTA: [Explore Collection].

The VIP Service Form (Mid-Page): * A clean, "Step-based" card.

Fields: [Select Suit Category] -> [Preferred Date/Time] -> [Location Pin].

Value Prop: "Our Master Tailor brings the showroom to your chambers."

Shop by Category Grid: * Four large high-res tiles: Courtroom Classics, The Boardroom, Formal Galas, and Executive Accessories.

Trust Bar: Logos of top-tier law firms or "As seen in" legal journals.

2. "All Products" / Shop Page
Goal: High-speed discovery with powerful filtering.

Top Bar: * Breadcrumbs: Home > Suits > All Products.

Active Filters Display: Small chips showing "Wool", "Size 40R", "Under ₹50,000" with "X" to remove.

Sort Dropdown: [Newest] [Price: Low to High] [Most Popular] [Rating: High to Low].

Left Sidebar (The Filter Engine):

Category: Accordion for Suits, Shirts, Ties, Belts, Cufflinks.

Price Range: A dual-point slider with manual input boxes.

Size: A grid of buttons (38, 40, 42, 44...).

Fabric: Checkboxes (100% Merino Wool, Silk Blend, Egyptian Cotton).

Fit: [Slim Fit] [Modern Fit] [Classic Fit].

Product Grid: * 3-column layout on desktop, 1-column on mobile.

Card UI: * Image with "Quick View" hover effect.

"Home Fitting Available" badge in the corner.

Price and star rating (e.g., ★ 4.8 | 120 Reviews).

3. Product Detail Page (PDP)
Goal: Sensory details and fit confidence.

Image Gallery (Left): Vertical thumbnails on the side, large main image with "Deep Zoom" capability to show fabric texture.

Product Info (Right):

Title: "The Solicitor's Charcoal Three-Piece."

Price: Clear display with "Or 3 interest-free payments" (Financing info).

Size Selection: Grid of sizes + [Size Guide] link.

The "VIP Handshake": A toggle switch:

( ) Standard Shipping

(x) Home Measurement & Sample Viewing (+₹999 - Refundable on purchase).

Fabric Composition: Bullet points describing the thread count and breathability.

Cross-Sell: "Pairs perfectly with our Silk Navy Tie."

4. The Cart & Checkout
Goal: Secure the transaction and schedule the visit.

Mini-Cart (Slide-out): Appears when an item is added. Shows subtotal and a progress bar for "Free Home Fitting" (e.g., "Add ₹2,000 more for free VIP service").

Checkout Page (One-Page Design):

Section 1: Contact & Address: Auto-fill for logged-in users.

Section 2: The Appointment: A calendar UI. If the user chose "Home Measurement," they pick their 3-hour window here.

Section 3: Payment: Integrated Razorpay frame. Options for UPI, Cards, and Netbanking.

Section 4: Summary: Final breakdown including the discount_total from the coupon applied.

5. User Dashboard: "The Lawyer's Vault"
Goal: Retention and easy re-ordering.

Measurement Profile: A visual silhouette of a suit showing the user's saved metrics (Chest, Waist, Inseam).

Status: "Verified by Tailor [Name] on [Date]."

Order History: List of past purchases with "Re-order this Fit" buttons.

Wishlist: A "Saved for Later" grid for accessories they might want for their next court date.

6. Global Elements (Nav & Footer)
Sticky Header: * Left: Hamburger menu (Mobile) / Category Links (Desktop).

Center: Minimalist Logo (Gold or Silver Serif font).

Right: Search icon, User Profile icon, and Cart icon with a red notification bubble.

Footer:

Columns: Company (About, Careers), Client Care (Returns, Shipping, FAQ), Legal (Privacy, Terms).

Newsletter: "Join the Inner Circle: Weekly style tips for the modern advocate."

UX Design Principles for this Case:
Mobile-First: Most lawyers will browse this while commuting or between hearings. The "Home Measurement" form must be thumb-friendly.

High-Contrast Typography: Use a combination of a classic Serif font (for headings) and a clean Sans-serif (for body text/data) to ensure readability and professional "weight."

Loading States: Use "Skeleton Screens" while your Hetzner backend fetches the product list to make the site feel instantaneous.
