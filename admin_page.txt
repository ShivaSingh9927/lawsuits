To manage your inventory, pricing, and stock status dynamically, you need a Merchant/Admin Dashboard. Since you are using Vercel for the frontend and Hetzner for the backend, this dashboard can be a protected route in your Next.js application that communicates with your Node.js API.

Here is the detailed design and functional plan for your Product Management System.

1. The "Product Inventory" Master View
This is the command center where you see everything at a glance.

Top Bar: Search bar (for SKU or Name), "Add New Product" button, and a "Global Sale" toggle (to apply a discount to an entire category).

The Table/Grid:

Product Thumbnail: A small 50x50 image.

Name & Category: (e.g., "The Barrister’s Navy" | Suits).

Stock Status: A color-coded badge. [Green: In Stock], [Yellow: Low Stock], [Red: Out of Stock].

Price: Shows current_price and compare_at_price.

Quick Actions: Edit icon, Delete (Trash) icon, and a "Visibility" toggle (to hide a product without deleting it).

2. The "Edit / Add Product" Page (The Form)
This page maps directly to your Postgres Schema. It should be divided into logical sections:

A. Basic Information
Product Name & Slug: Auto-generate the slug from the name.

Description: A Rich Text Editor (for bolding fabric specs).

Category Dropdown: Pulls from your categories table.

B. Pricing & Discounts (The "Freedom" Section)
Base Price: The regular selling price.

Compare At Price: Put the higher MSRP here to show a "Sale" tag on the frontend.

Cost per Item: (Internal only) To calculate your profit margins.

C. Variants & Inventory (The "Size/Color" Matrix)
Since suits have many sizes, use a Variant Manager:

A table where you can add rows for each size (38R, 40R, 42L).

SKU Generator: Unique ID for each size.

Stock Quantity: An input field to update numbers after a restock.

Out of Stock Toggle: A manual override switch to mark a specific size as unavailable even if the database thinks there is 1 left.

D. Media Upload
Drag & Drop Zone: Upload images to your S3/Object Storage.

Thumbnail Selector: A radio button to choose which image is the "Primary" view.

3. Backend Logic for the Admin Panel
To ensure these operations are secure and fast on your Hetzner VPS:

Authorization (RBAC): You must implement Role-Based Access Control. Only users with the ROLE_ADMIN should be able to access these endpoints. Use a JWT (JSON Web Token) with a role claim.

Audit Logs: Whenever you change a price or delete a product, write a record to an admin_logs table. This is vital for a business to track who changed what and when.

Image Optimization: When you upload a high-res suit photo, have your backend resize it into three versions: thumbnail, mobile, and desktop to keep your Vercel frontend fast.

Soft Deletes: Instead of permanently deleting a product from Postgres (which would break your order_items history), use a deleted_at timestamp. If it’s not null, the product is hidden.

4. Special Feature: "Bulk Price Updater"
Since you mentioned the freedom of price changes based on sales, you should include a tool that allows you to:

"Increase price of all Accessories by 10%."

"Mark all Slim Fit Suits as 'Sale' with a 20% discount."

This sends a single UPDATE query to your Postgres database, saving you from editing 50 products one by one.

5. Managing the "Home Fitting" Service from Admin
You also need a view to manage the Appointments:

Calendar View: See which tailors are booked and where.

Assignment: Drag and drop an order onto a staff member to assign the home visit.
